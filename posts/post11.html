Top Hardwood Choices for New England

1. Red Oak

A classic choice for a reason, Red Oak is known for its durability and resistance to wear and tear. It has a Janka hardness rating of around 1290, making it tough enough for high-traffic areas. Its strong grain pattern also helps to hide minor scratches and dents. More importantly, Red Oak is relatively stable and adapts well to changes in humidity, which is a key consideration in Massachusetts.

2. White Oak

Slightly harder than Red Oak (Janka rating of 1360), White Oak is another excellent option. It has a subtler, straighter grain and is more water-resistant due to its closed-grain structure. This makes it a great choice for kitchens and entryways. White Oakâ€™s stability and classic look make it a popular choice for homes throughout the greater Boston area.

3. Maple

Maple is one of the hardest domestic woods (Janka rating of 1450), making it incredibly durable and resistant to dents. Its light, creamy color and subtle grain pattern can brighten up any room, creating a clean and modern look.
