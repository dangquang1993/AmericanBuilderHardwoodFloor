Understanding Hardwood Flooring Grades

Hardwood flooring is graded based on the natural characteristics visible in the wood. These grades don't indicate quality or durability - they simply describe the appearance. Here are the most common grades you'll encounter:

Grade Appearance Best For
Clear Nearly uniform appearance with minimal knots or color variations Formal spaces, modern designs
Select Some color variation but few knots Traditional to contemporary styles
Character Blend of select and #1 common features Balance of refinement and character
#1 Common Moderate color variation and small knots Casual, rustic, or cottage styles
#2 Common Pronounced color variation and larger knots Very rustic or reclaimed looks

How to Choose the Right Grade

When selecting a hardwood grade, consider:

  • Your design style: Clean grades suit modern spaces, while character grades work well in rustic or traditional homes
  • Room size: Higher grades (clear/select) can make small rooms feel larger
  • Maintenance: More character means minor scratches are less noticeable
  • Budget: Clear grades typically cost more than character grades

Hardwood Flooring Finishes Explained

The finish you choose affects your floor's appearance, durability, and maintenance requirements. Modern hardwood floors come with two main types of finishes:

Surface Finishes (Polyurethane)

  • Types: Oil-based or water-based polyurethane
  • Appearance: Plastic-like protective layer
  • Durability: Highly resistant to stains and wear
  • Maintenance: Easy to clean, doesn't require waxing
  • Best for: High-traffic areas, families with kids/pets

Penetrating Finishes (Oil)

  • Types: Hardwax oils, natural oils
  • Appearance: Natural matte look that enhances wood grain
  • Durability: Softer surface that develops a patina
  • Maintenance: Requires periodic re-oiling
  • Best for: Low-traffic areas, rustic or natural looks

Sheen Levels

Within these finish types, you can choose different sheen levels:

  • Matte: 10-25% gloss - hides scratches well, modern look
  • Satin: 30-40% gloss - most popular balanced option
  • Semi-gloss: 40-55% gloss - shows more imperfections
  • Gloss: 60-90% gloss - formal, high-maintenance

Matching Grades and Finishes to Your Lifestyle

For Busy Households (Kids/Pets)

  • Choose a character grade (scratches blend in better)
  • Select a matte or satin polyurethane finish for maximum durability
  • Consider harder wood species like hickory or maple

For Formal Living Spaces

  • Opt for clear or select grade for a uniform look
  • Choose a satin or semi-gloss finish for elegance
  • Consider oak or walnut for classic appeal

For Rustic or Farmhouse Styles

  • Select #1 or #2 common grade for maximum character
  • Consider a hand-scraped texture with matte finish
  • Look at oak or hickory with natural color variations
