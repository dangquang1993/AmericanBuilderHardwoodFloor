2. Protect from Scratches

Preventing scratches is essential for maintaining the appearance of your hardwood floors. Follow these tips:

  • Use Furniture Pads: Place felt pads under the legs of furniture to prevent scratches when moving chairs or tables.
  • Avoid High Heels: High heels and cleats can dent or scratch your floors. Encourage family members and guests to remove such footwear indoors.
  • Trim Pet Nails: Keep your petsâ€™ nails trimmed to minimize scratches from their claws.

3. Control Humidity Levels

Hardwood floors are sensitive to changes in humidity, which can cause them to expand or contract. To prevent warping or gaps:

  • Use a Humidifier or Dehumidifier: Maintain indoor humidity levels between 30% and 50% to keep your floors stable.
  • Avoid Excessive Moisture: Clean up spills immediately to prevent water damage.

5. Use Area Rugs and Mats

Area rugs and mats can protect your hardwood floors in high-traffic areas:

  • Entryways: Place mats at entryways to trap dirt and moisture before it reaches your floors.
  • High-Traffic Areas: Use rugs in areas like hallways and living rooms to reduce wear and tear.
