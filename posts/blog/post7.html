Wide Plank vs. Narrow Plank Hardwood

How to Choose the Best Option for Your Home

Expert Advice from American Builder Hardwood Floor

When selecting hardwood flooring, one of the biggest decisions homeowners face is choosing between wide plank and narrow plank hardwood. Both options offer distinct advantages in terms of aesthetics, durability, and installation. In this guide, we'll compare wide vs. narrow plank hardwood and help you decide which is best for your home.

1. What's the Difference?

Wide Plank Hardwood

  • Width: Typically 5 inches or wider (up to 12+ inches).
  • Appearance: Creates a modern, open, and rustic look with fewer seams.
  • Best For: Large rooms, open-concept spaces, and homes with a farmhouse or contemporary style.

Narrow Plank Hardwood

  • Width: Usually 2Â¼ to 4 inches wide (traditional strip flooring).
  • Appearance: Offers a classic, refined, and uniform look with more linear seams.
  • Best For: Smaller rooms, traditional homes, and spaces where a timeless aesthetic is desired.

2. Pros and Cons of Wide Plank Hardwood

Pros:

  • Modern & Spacious Feel - Fewer seams make rooms appear larger.
  • Showcases Wood Grain - Highlights the natural beauty of hardwood.
  • Trendy & Luxurious - Popular in upscale and rustic designs.

Cons:

  • Higher Cost - Wider planks often cost more due to material requirements.
  • More Prone to Movement - Can expand/contract more with humidity changes.
  • Not Ideal for All Subfloors - Requires a very flat subfloor to prevent warping.

At American Builder Hardwood Floor, we ensure proper installation to minimize movement issues.

3. Pros and Cons of Narrow Plank Hardwood

Pros:

  • Classic & Timeless - Works well in both traditional and modern homes.
  • More Stable - Less prone to expansion/contraction than wide planks.
  • Budget-Friendly - Often more affordable than wide planks.

Cons:

  • More Seams - Can make small rooms feel busier.
  • Less Dramatic Grain Visibility - Narrow planks show less wood character.

For homeowners who prefer durability and a traditional look, narrow planks are a great choice.

4. Which Should You Choose?

Choose Wide Plank If You...

  • Want a modern, open, or rustic look.
  • Have large rooms or open floor plans.
  • Love bold wood grain patterns.

Choose Narrow Plank If You...

  • Prefer a classic, traditional aesthetic.
  • Need a budget-friendly and stable option.
  • Have smaller rooms or uneven subfloors.
